What is Ohm's Law?

Ohm's Law is one of the most fundamental principles in electrical engineering, formulated by German physicist Georg Ohm in 1827.

It states that the current flowing through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points, and inversely proportional to the resistance between them.
